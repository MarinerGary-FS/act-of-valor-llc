# Conversion Logic — Act of Valor

## Core Objective
Drive immediate user action during high-stress situations.

This site must:
- Reduce hesitation instantly
- Provide clear next steps
- Prioritize phone calls above all other actions

---

## USER STATE ASSUMPTION

Users are:
- In distress
- Time-sensitive
- Not comparing multiple vendors
- Seeking immediate help

All logic must reduce thinking and increase action.

---

## PRIMARY CONVERSION PRIORITY

1. Phone Call (highest priority)
2. Form Submission
3. Chat Engagement

All design and logic must reinforce this hierarchy.

---

## TIME-TO-ACTION TARGET

User must be guided to a decision within:
5–20 seconds (ideal)
30 seconds maximum

---

## CTA STRUCTURE (MANDATORY)

CTA must appear in the following locations:

- Hero section (above the fold)
- Sticky header (persistent)
- After each service section
- After trust/credentials section
- Before footer
- Footer

---

## CTA DESIGN PRIORITY

Primary CTA:
Call Now — Immediate Help

Secondary CTA:
Request Same-Day Service

Rules:
- Primary CTA must always visually dominate
- Secondary CTA supports, never competes

---

## PHONE DOMINANCE SYSTEM

- Sticky header with click-to-call
- Phone number always visible
- Tap-to-call enabled on all devices
- Phone CTA repeated throughout page

On mobile:
- Must be thumb-accessible at all times
- Must require zero friction to initiate call

---

## FORM STRATEGY (LOW FRICTION)

Fields:
- Name
- Phone
- What happened
- Location

Rules:
- No unnecessary fields
- No multi-step forms
- No delays

Goal:
Capture lead in under 15 seconds

---

## AUTOMATION FLOW (POST-SUBMISSION)

Immediately after form submission:

1. SMS sent instantly:
"Act of Valor here. We received your request—are you safe right now?"

2. Internal alert to team (real-time)

3. Optional:
Auto-call trigger for high urgency cases

---

## FAILURE RECOVERY (CRITICAL)

If user does NOT call or submit:

- Chat prompt appears after delay
- CTA emphasis increases subtly
- User is re-offered clear action

Example:
"Need help right now? We're here."

Goal:
Re-engage without overwhelming

---

## CHAT ENGAGEMENT LOGIC

Chat must:
- Offer help, not interrupt
- Appear after short delay

Questions:
- What happened?
- Where are you located?
- Is anyone in danger?

Chat must route into lead capture system

---

## TRUST INJECTION STRATEGY

Trust elements must appear BEFORE hesitation occurs:

- Credentials / certifications (strongest trust signal)
- Law enforcement relationships
- Testimonials
- Local Atlanta presence

Placement:
- Early and repeated throughout the site

---

## URGENCY BALANCE

The site must:
- Communicate urgency
- Maintain calm tone

Avoid:
- Aggressive language
- Fear-based messaging

Use:
- Reassuring authority
- Clear guidance

---

## SEO + CONVERSION INTEGRATION

- Services link to resources
- Resources reinforce authority
- Location references improve relevance

All links must support both:
- User clarity
- Search visibility

---

## MOBILE-FIRST CONVERSION RULES

- One-thumb navigation
- Large tap targets
- No clutter
- Fast load speed

Mobile experience must outperform desktop

---

## PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENT

- Fast load time is mandatory
- No heavy scripts that delay interaction
- Prioritize speed over visual effects

---

## FINAL GOAL

User lands → feels understood → takes action immediately

Every element must support:
CALL → SUBMIT → CONNECT
